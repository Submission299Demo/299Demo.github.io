Supplementary Materials: Flight Simulator

Below are videos illustrating our flight simulator's performance at various injected network latency levels: 0ms, 100ms, and 400ms. Overall, the experience remains smooth and responsive at latency levels of 0ms and 100ms. However, when operating at 400ms latency, certain simulation aspects become less than ideal.

To offer a comprehensive view, we have zoomed in on one specific area where the simulation experience is notably compromised at 400ms latency. For a clearer comparison, we present this segment side-by-side with its corresponding portion at 0ms latency (shown on the left). This side-by-side comparison aims to help readers better understand how varying levels of latency can impact the overall user experience.

Flight Simulator: 0ms latency

This is a video of our flight simulator running fast2 dataset with no injected network latency. As can be empirically observed, the visual experience is smooth and pleasent.

Flight Simulator: 100ms injected network latency

This is a video of our flight simulator running fast2 dataset with 100ms injected network latency. As can be empirically observed, offloading at a typical network latencies, the visual experience is still smooth and pleasent.

Flight Simulator: 400ms injected network latency

This is a video of our flight simulator running fast2 dataset with 400ms injected network latency. As can be empirically observed, offloading operations at this level of latency result in noticeable jitter, thereby compromising the visual experience and diminishing its overall quality.

Side-by-Side Comparison

We present a side-by-side comparison of video segments showcasing a small portion of the fast2 trajectory in Flight Simulator. On the left, the simulation operates under 0 ms of network latency, while on the right, it operates under an injected latency of 400 ms. This juxtaposition serves to empirically highlight the differential effects of latency on the quality of the visual experience.
