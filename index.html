Supplementary Materials

Below are videos illustrating our flight simulator's performance at various network latency levels: 0ms, 100ms, and 400ms. Overall, the experience remains smooth and responsive at latency levels of 0ms and 100ms. However, when operating at 400ms latency, certain simulation aspects become less than ideal.

To offer a comprehensive view, we have zoomed in on one specific area where the simulation experience is notably compromised at 400ms latency. For a clearer comparison, we present this segment side-by-side with its corresponding portion at 0ms latency (shown on the left). This side-by-side comparison aims to help readers better understand how varying levels of latency can impact the overall user experience.

Flight Simulator: Offloading head tracking with 100ms latency

We show that offloading head tracking works well, without perceptible jitters at typical network latencies(100ms) in Flight Simulator.

Flight Simulator: Local head tracking

We show that when running with local head tracking, the user experience is very smooth in Flight Simulator.

Flight Simulator: Offloading head tracking with 400ms Latency

We show that when offloading tracking with latency of 400ms, parts of the rendered graphics start to see perceptible jitters in Flight Simulator.
